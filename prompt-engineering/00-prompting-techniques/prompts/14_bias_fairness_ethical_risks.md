# 14 — Bias, Fairness & Ethical Risks

## What it means

Bias, fairness, and ethical risk topics help us think about whether the model is making unfair, harmful, or irrelevant judgments.

## Main idea

A model should not make decisions or summaries using unrelated personal assumptions.

It should focus on:
- relevant evidence
- fair treatment
- respectful language
- balanced reasoning

## Why this matters

Without fairness and ethics guardrails, a model may:
- make assumptions based on identity signals
- use biased wording
- unfairly judge career gaps or personal background
- produce low-quality or harmful decision support

With fairness and ethics guardrails, the output becomes more responsible and job-relevant.

## Example use case

A hiring support assistant should evaluate:
- experience
- skills
- education
- relevant qualifications

It should not make assumptions based on:
- name
- gender
- age
- religion
- ethnicity
- unrelated personal background

## Key idea

A fair prompt keeps the model focused on relevant evidence instead of bias-prone assumptions.
